A — Serif Authority

Lora (serif) for ALL headings, labels, and numbers. Inter (sans) only for body text and small metadata. Creates a clear two-voice system: serif = important, sans = supportive. Numbers in serif feel weighty.

B — Serif Headings, Sans Everything Else

Serif reserved strictly for page-level headings. Section titles, stats, labels all in sans-serif with tracking and weight to create hierarchy. Keeps the current pattern but with sharper contrast between levels.

C — Italic Serif Voice

Serif italic for headings and featured numbers — adds calligraphic energy. Sans for labels and body. The italic gives movement and personality while staying elegant. Section headings in serif regular (not italic) for quieter moments.
